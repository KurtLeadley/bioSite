Building

After retiring around 1991, Roger decided it was time to make a house. Roger and his wife Pauline lived in the old house, while making the new house. The only catch was that the old house was exactly where the new house was going to be. So Roger made a plan to build the house third by third. Along the way, my father helped out and a few other people did as well, but Roger was the mastermind and day to day operations of the whole thing. Many many years were spent where he worked on the house alone. This house was not built over night.

During the Summer, and sometimes over the weekend during the Winter, I would come over to visit. I got to see the house being built piece by piece. I was only 6 or 7 years old when it started. I remember Grandpa showing me a couple things along the way. Some of those screws and nails holding that house up are from me. If you look through the slideshow, you can see how the house was built in sections. Third by third, day by day. There was a time where staying overnight meant sleeping in the garage or climbing up a ladder that was outside to the upstairs bedroom. It was a lot of fun actually.
